Jollof recipe

Description

Jollof rice is a rich and incredbly tasty one-pot meal popular in the West African regions of Senegal, Ghana, Nigeria, Togo, Liberia, and Sierra Leon. It has its origins from the wollof people of Senegal, West Africa.

Though each country and also, households within these countries have their own way of making this sumptuous dish, the common practice is to cook rice in a flavorful and tasty tomato stew. Some countries, especially Ghana and Nigeria have created what is popularly known as the "jollof wars" out of this delicacy; an unending "war" about which country cooks it best. But most Africans will agree with me that there is no absolute right or wrong way of making the jollof dish.

Ingredients

  • Uncooked rice.
  • fresh tomatoes and/or tomato paste.
  • Onions.
  • Garlic.
  • Ginger
  • Cooking oil.
  • Meat broth/water.
  • Spices of your choice. e.g. bayleaf, thyme, curry powder, black pepper, etc.
  • Salt to taste.
  • Stock cube to taste.
  • Vegetables like carrot, peas, green pepper, etc.(optional).
  • Scotch bonnets (if you like your food hot).

Steps:

  1. Blend your onions, ginger, scotch bonnets and garlic. Put it aside.
  2. Heat your cooking oil in a cooking pot. Put your stove on low heat.
  3. Add the ginger, garlic, scotch bonnet and onion mixture to the heatedf oil. Stir for about a minute or until golden brown.
  4. Blend or dice your fresh tomatoes and add it to the pot. you can choose to add a little of some store bought tomato paste to give it a rch color. Some may choose to use only fresh tomatoes or only tomato paste. it all boils down to personal preference.
  5. Stir under medium heat for about 5min.
  6. Add your choice of spices, i.e thyme, bayleaf, currypowder, etc. You can also add your stock cube and salt at this stage.
  7. Stir for another 5min under medium heat.
  8. Add your broth/water accordingly (the amount of rice determines how much water to add.
  9. Rinse the rice if you don't want it starchy. Do not parboil if you don't want your jollof to turn out soggy.
  10. Add your rice to the cooking pot and stir well.
  11. Cover the pot and let the it cook for 10-15min.
  12. After 15min you can check your rice to see whether it is well cooked or it still needs some water.
  13. If the rice is still a bit hard, you can add a little water to the bottom to avoid the ingredients seeping to the bottom of the pot and reducing its yumminess.
  14. Chech again after 5-7min. If the water is absorbed and you feel the texture is right for you, then your jollof rice is ready.
  15. Enjoy!
